1. Listener rule (not only default action)
ECS needs a listener rule ARN it can update during deployments. A listener default_action alone is not enough.
You need an aws_lb_listener_rule on the production listener (port 80) that forwards to the blue target group. ECS will change this rule to point to green when shifting traffic.
2. ECS service advanced_configuration
The ECS service must include an advanced_configuration block in its load_balancer config:
alternate_target_group_arn – green target group ARN
production_listener_rule – ARN of the listener rule above (not the listener)
role_arn – IAM role that allows ECS to manage load balancer rules
3. deployment_configuration with blue/green
The service needs deployment settings for blue/green:
deployment_configuration with strategy = "BLUE_GREEN" (or equivalent)
bake_time_in_minutes – how long both blue and green run before blue is terminated
maximum_percent / minimum_healthy_percent – e.g. 200 / 100 for blue/green
4. IAM role for load balancer management
ECS needs an IAM role with ecs.amazonaws.com as the principal and the attached policy:
arn:aws:iam::aws:policy/AmazonECSInfrastructureRolePolicyForLoadBalancers
5. Optional: test listener
For testing green before production traffic shift, you can keep port 8080 forwarding to the green target group. The optional test_listener_rule in advanced_configuration can point to that traffic path.
